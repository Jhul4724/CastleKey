PASSWORDS 101

  • What is a Password?

    • A password is a secret sequence of characters, or phrase that is used to authenticate a user
    • Nowadays, passwords are everywhere; everyone has passwords for everything
      • Governement Services
      • Academic Institutions
      • Electronic Devices
      • Social Media Accounts
      • Online Gaming Accounts

  • Compromised Passwords

    • If a password is compromised, there is a wide variety of impacts that can occur, including some that may cause a huge impact
      • Disruption to Critical Infrastructures
      • Identity Theft
      • Data Loss
      • Reputation Damage
    • Furthermore, it can lead to "pivoting"
      • A compromised account can contain information that can then compromise another account
      • A compromised account can be used to login into other accounts (such as Google)

  • Choosing a Secure Password

    • In order to protect their information, users have to choose a hard-to-guess password
    • There are many criteria that can determine a strong password
      • Password Length
      • Avoid Common Words & Sequences
      • Use Numbers
      • Use Symbols
    • The most effective criterion is the password length (CCCS recommends creating passwords of length 15+)

  • Other Good Practices

    • In addition, there are other good practices in order to further protect your information
      • Avoid Reusing Passwords
      • Avoid Common Words
      • Avoid Personal Information
      • Use Multi-Factor Authentication (Preferably Authenticator Apps)
      • Use Randomized Long Passwords
      • Use a Password Manager
      • Sign Up for Have I Been Pwned (HIBP) Notifications
